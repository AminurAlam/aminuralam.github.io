Why this blog "wastes" so much "horizontal space" is a common complaint I get about its "design". Well, that and the cheesy inclusion of teal and houndstooth, for which I offer no apology.

Feel free to accept or ignore whichever of these explanations you prefer, or that conform to your worldview:

- The conventional wisdom is that text lines should be between 50–80 characters wide for readability. Studies on this aren't as robust or scientific as I'd like, but I can speak from personal experience that longer lines are frustrating to read (and can trigger headaches in those of us predisposed to such fun).

- This is a blog of posts in descending publication order. Having them in one column, with those readable lines, keeps things simple.

- I've always done it this way. Inertia, and all that.

- I tile windows, because maximising on widescreens doesn't make sense to me. Your mileage may vary.

- The Bird is The Word.

- I am not a professional designer, and only know enough to be dangerous.
